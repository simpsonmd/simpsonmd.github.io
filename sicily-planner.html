Driving Notes for Sicily

You're renting a car — smart move for flexibility. A few things to know before you hit the road:

  • Taormina ZTL: The historic center is a restricted zone (ZTL). Park at Lumbi or Porta Catania parking lots and walk in — or take the cable car. Cameras are active 24/7.
  • Catania ZTL: City center has ZTL zones, especially around Via Etnea and Piazza del Duomo. Use peripheral parking + walk or taxi.
  • Taormina → Catania: ~1hr on the A18/E45 autostrada. Straightforward drive.
  • Parking tip: Search "Parcheggio Lumbi Taormina" — it connects directly to town by escalators and is the easiest option.

Taormina → Catania (~1 hour)
Head south on the A18. Scenic coastal drive with Etna always to the west. Park at a hotel garage or use one of the peripheral lots near the historic center — don't try to drive into Piazza del Duomo.

Explore
Fontana dell'Elefante & Piazza del Duomo
The black lava elephant is Catania's symbol — ancient, quirky, beloved. The piazza around it has Baroque architecture built from Etna's lava stone. Grab a granita at one of the bars on the square.
Free Open 24 hours

Morning
Morning granita at Piazza del Duomo
The Sicilian morning: brioche con gelato or granita di mandorla (almond granita). Grab it from one of the bars right on the piazza and watch the city wake up.
10AM
Optional
La Pescheria — Fish Market (if open)
Catania's famous fish market runs weekday mornings near Piazza del Duomo. Chaotic, aromatic, theatrical — vendors shouting, swordfish on ice, locals bartering. Even if you're not buying, it's a full sensory experience. Goes until ~1PM.
Weekday mornings only Free to browse
1PM
Farewell Lunch
Putì or Tantikkia — Last meal
Two excellent options for a final lunch. Putì has a small curated menu of whatever's fresh — sea urchin pasta, oysters, horse steak. Tantikkia is great for octopus and swordfish with a lively outdoor terrace. Both on Via Etnea / nearby.
€€ Both open for lunch
